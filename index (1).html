About these scores & scales — please read The ratings on this dashboard blend official data with editorial estimates. Headline workforce shares (agriculture, manufacturing, construction, trade) come from official labour surveys; finer service-sector splits are estimates. The 1–5 hiring-demand scores, the AI-impact categories (🔴/🟠/🟢) and their 1–5 intensity, the growth labels, the salary bands, and the 2-year forecast figures, ranges and Low/Med/High sensitivity ratings are Claude's own composite judgements — synthesised from the cited sources to convey relative direction and magnitude, not precise measurement. Every scale is ordinal and indicative: a "4/5" is not twice a "2/5", and forecast percentages are scenario estimates, not predictions. Use these for comparison and discussion only — they are not statistical, financial, investment, policy or hiring advice. ⚡ The AI surge

Globally, big-data specialists, fintech engineers and AI/ML specialists are the three fastest-growing roles in percentage terms. Tech skills, AI and cybersecurity are projected to grow in importance faster than any other skillset by 2030.

🌐 Net +78M jobs by 2030

The WEF Future of Jobs Report models 170M jobs created and 92M displaced by 2030 — a churn of 22% of formal jobs, for a net gain of about 7%. Care, tech and green roles lead the growth.

📉 Roles in retreat

Clerical and routine roles are shrinking fastest worldwide — cashiers, data-entry clerks, admin secretaries, bank tellers and postal clerks. The same automation pressures touch India and New Zealand.
